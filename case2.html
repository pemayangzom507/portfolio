Product Overview

The Art Museum App provide an all-in-one solution for museum-goers to explore current and upcoming exhibitions, view events, and easily access essential museum information. The platform allows users to schedule visits, purchase tickets, and stay updated with museum happenings through a seamless, responsive design that enhances both user engagement and museum management.

Category

Mobile App Design

Project Duration

20th September - 7th October

My Role

UX Designer

My Responsibilty

User research, digital and paper wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, and design iteration.

Target Users

Museum visitors, art enthusiasts, event-goers

User Pain Points

Lack of Information

Museum visitors often find it challenging to access up-to-date information about exhibitions, events, and schedules.

No App

Currently, in Bhutan we don't have such app to schecule a visit to any of the museums or art gallery.

Slow Payment Processing

If payment transactions take too long, users may abandon their bookings in frustration.

Persona

Problem Statement

Sonam is an art enthusiast who loves visiting exhibitions on weekends but finds it difficult to stay informed about current events and upcoming exhibits. Sonam needs an app that makes it easy to discover new exhibitions, plan visits, and book tickets efficiently. The app should ensure an enjoyable experience without the hassle of navigating through multiple menus.

Problem Statement

Lhamo enjoys attending art events but is frustrated by complicated museum websites. She values quick access to exhibition details and the ability to schedule a visit with minimal effort. Lhamo needs a responsive website and app that allow him to seamlessly plan visits and purchase tickets.

User Journey Map

Wireframe

Paper Wireframe

The paper wireframe showcases the fundamental structure of the public art museum app and website. This visual outline illustrates how users will explore exhibitions, access event details, and easily book tickets. Focusing on intuitive navigation, the wireframe ensures that users can browse museum content and schedule visits without hassle.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

The low-fidelity prototype introduces a basic interactive model for the museum's app and website. This version outlines the user flow, helping visitors browse exhibitions and events, book tickets, and schedule visits. It emphasizes ease of navigation and streamlined design to meet user needs.

High-Fidelity Wireframe

The high-fidelity prototype represents the polished, interactive version of the museum's platform. Visitors can easily browse exhibitions, view event schedules, and book tickets, while enjoying a responsive design that adapts to both mobile and desktop devices. This version ensures a seamless user experience, with secure payment processing for ticket purchases.

Usuability Study

I conducted two usability tests—one for the low-fidelity prototype and one for the high-fidelity version of the museum platform. The studies highlighted areas for improvement, ensuring that the final product aligned with user expectations and provided a smooth experience for both museum patrons and staff.

Round 1 Findings

1. Difficulty in Scheduling Visits Users found it challenging to locate exhibition information quickly.


2. Confusing Booking Process Users encountered confusion when trying to schedule visits or book tickets.

Round 2 Findings

1. Navigation to Events Users needed clearer paths to event schedules and booking pages.


2. Design Consistency Inconsistent color schemes across pages led to user confusion..

Takeaways

Impact

The app and website made it easier for museum patrons to engage with exhibitions and events, resulting in higher visitor satisfaction and increased ticket sales. The platform also streamlined museum operations by offering a user-friendly system for patrons to schedule visits and access museum information

What I Learned

I honed my skills in creating responsive designs that balance accessibility and aesthetics. Additionally, I learned the importance of user feedback in refining mobile application to meet diverse user needs.
