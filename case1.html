Product Overview

The Seat Reservation App simplifies the movie ticket booking process by offering real-time seat availability, showtime details, and an intuitive interface. It enables users to quickly secure their preferred seats, reducing frustration and enhancing overall satisfaction. The app improves both user experience and theater operations by providing up-to-date information and seamless payment options.

Category

Mobile App Design

Project Duration

3rd September - 17th September

My Role

UX Designer

My Responsibilty

Interviewing, digital and paper wireframing, low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototyping, usability testing, and iterating on designs.

Target Users

Movie Goers

User Pain Points

Ambiguous Status

Users struggle to understand which seats are available versus booked, especially if the color coding or symbols are not intuitive.

Limited Payment Methods

Users may be frustrated if their preferred payment options (e.g., digital wallets, credit cards) are not supported

Slow Payment Processing

If payment transactions take too long, users may abandon their bookings in frustration.

Persona

Problem Statement

Karma is a moviegoer who enjoys spending weekends at the cinema with his friends but struggles with existing movie reservation apps. These apps often fail to display accurate seat availability and are difficult to navigate, leading to frustration and missed opportunities. Karma needs a user-friendly, intuitive seat reservation app that allows him to quickly view seat options and secure the best seats for his family and friends without hassle. The app should provide a smooth, reliable booking experience to accommodate his busy schedule.

Problem Statement

Passang, a 20-year-old spontaneous moviegoer, frequently plans last-minute movie nights with friends but is often frustrated by complex and slow-loading reservation apps. She values a fast, straightforward app that clearly displays seat availability and allows her to make quick bookings with minimal effort. Passang needs an efficient and user-friendly solution that simplifies the movie reservation process.

Paper Wireframe

By focusing on a simple and intuitive design, the wireframe helps identify key interactions and user needs, ensuring that the app effectively supports a seamless ticket purchasing experience.

The prototype emphasizes a straightforward design that simplifies the ticket purchasing process, ensuring users can navigate through the app with ease while retaining essential functionality.

Usuability Study

I conducted two rounds of usability testing, one on the low-fidelity prototype and another on the high-fidelity version of the seat reservation app. These usability tests were crucial for identifying how users interact with the app and understanding their pain points. The insights from these studies guided me in refining the design to meet user needs more effectively.

Round 1 Findings

1. Too many steps to book seats Users felt overwhelmed by the number of steps involved in selecting seats and completing the booking.


2. Confusing seat availability indicators The seat status (available, booked, etc.) wasn’t clear, leading to confusion.

Round 2 Findings

1. Unclear navigation flow to payment After selecting seats, users were unsure how to proceed to the payment page.


2. Inconsistent color scheme The color scheme for the available and unavailable seats was hard to distinguish, requiring a clearer contrast.

Takeaways

Impact

Designing the seat reservation app improved both user experience and theater operations. It made the booking process more convenient, reducing wait times and manual effort. Users enjoyed choosing preferred seats and received real-time updates, leading to higher satisfaction and loyalty.

What I Learned

I gained experience balancing simplicity and functionality, prioritizing accessibility, refining designs through user feedback, collaborating effectively with developers, and focusing on mobile-first design for a responsive user experience.
